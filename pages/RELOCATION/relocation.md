**Power of Attorney**  
A power of attorney between a home buyer and his representative gives the latter the legal authority to act on behalf of the buyer in all matters relating to the sale of the property. In essence, the buyer delegates his authority to the representative to perform certain actions in his place.

What is it used for when buying a home?

The power of attorney is useful in several situations:

If the buyer resides abroad:  
If the buyer lives outside of Spain, the power of attorney allows a representative in Spain to manage all the necessary procedures for the purchase of the home, without the buyer having to travel constantly.

If the buyer cannot be present at all the procedures:  
Due to work, illness or other circumstances, the buyer may not be able to be present at all the appointments and procedures necessary for the purchase. The power of attorney allows his representative to act on his behalf.

To speed up the process:  
Having a representative with a power of attorney can speed up the buying and selling process, as the representative can make decisions and take action on behalf of the buyer without the need for constant approval.

For added security:  
In some cases, the buyer may prefer to have a representative, such as a lawyer or property manager, handle the sale for added security and to ensure that everything is done correctly according to the law.

What actions can the representative with a power of attorney perform?

The power of attorney should clearly specify what actions the representative can perform. This may include:

\- Signing contracts: The representative can sign the sales contract on behalf of the buyer.  
\- Managing financing: The representative can manage the mortgage or any other type of financing.  
\- Making payments: The representative can make the necessary payments on behalf of the buyer.  
\- Managing documentation: The representative can manage the necessary documentation for the sale.  
\- Attending appointments: The representative can attend appointments with the seller, the notary, etc.

It is important:  
The power of attorney must be granted before a notary and must be drafted accurately, clearly specifying the powers of the representative. A poorly drafted power of attorney can lead to legal problems. It is advisable to consult with a lawyer to ensure that the power of attorney is correctly drafted and covers all the needs of the buyer.

**NIE number**  
The Foreigner Identification Number (NIE) is a tax identification code assigned to foreign citizens who reside in Spain or who need to carry out legal or administrative procedures in the country. It is essentially the equivalent of the Tax Identification Number (NIF) for Spaniards, but for foreigners.

What is the NIE for?

The NIE is necessary for a wide variety of procedures and activities in Spain, including:

\- Buying or selling a property: It is essential to carry out any real estate transaction.  
\- Opening a bank account: Most banks require a NIE to open an account.  
\- Working in Spain: Necessary to obtain an employment contract and pay Social Security contributions.  
\- Driving in Spain: To obtain or renew a driving license.  
\- Obtaining a health card: To access the public health system.  
\- Enrolling in university: To enroll in university studies.  
\- Setting up a company: To register a company in Spain.  
\- Declare taxes: To file your income tax return.  
\- Obtain a visa or residence permit: In many cases, the NIE is a requirement to obtain a visa or residence permit.

In short, the NIE is an essential document for any foreigner who wants to live, work or carry out legal procedures in Spain. Without it, many administrative procedures would be impossible.

**Opening Bank account**  
A bank account in Spain for foreigners with residency is essential to fully integrate into the daily life of the country. It allows you to carry out a wide range of financial transactions and management.

What can a foreigner do with a bank account in Spain?

\- Receive your salary: If you work in Spain, you can receive your salary directly into your account.  
\- Pay taxes: It is essential to pay taxes, both national and regional.  
\- Pay services: Pay utility bills (electricity, water, gas, telephone, internet), rent, community of owners, etc.  
\- Make online purchases: Buy goods and services online safely.  
\- Receive international transfers: Receive money from family or friends abroad.  
\- Manage your savings and investments: Manage your savings and investments in Spain.  
\- Receive social benefits: If you are entitled to any social benefits, you can receive them in your account.  
\- Use debit and credit cards: Access a wide network of ATMs and businesses that accept cards.  
\- Pay with Bizum: Use the Bizum app to make quick and easy transfers to other users.

Important things foreigners should know about bank accounts in Spain:

\- Required documentation: To open a bank account, you will need your NIE (Foreigner Identification Number), passport or ID card, and possibly other documents depending on the bank.  
\- Residency requirements: Some banks may require a minimum amount of time of residence in Spain.  
\- Fees: Banks charge fees for different services, such as account maintenance, transfers, credit or debit cards, etc. It is important to compare the fees of different banks before opening an account.  
\- Taxes: Interest generated by your savings may be subject to taxes.  
\- Language: Although many banks offer services in English, it is useful to have a basic knowledge of Spanish to make transactions easier.  
\- Security: Protect your banking information and take steps to prevent fraud.

 **Empadronamiento**  
The registration certificate in Spain is the administrative registry of the inhabitants of a municipality. That is, it is the official registry that proves that a person resides in a certain place. It is a municipal registry, not a state registry.

What is the purpose of the registration certificate? (EMPADRONAMIENTO)

The registration certificate serves as proof of residence in a municipality and is necessary for a wide variety of administrative procedures, such as:

\- Obtaining a health card: To access public health care.  
\- Enrolling in an educational center: To enroll children in schools or institutes.  
\- Applying for social assistance: To access aid and subsidies from the city council.  
\- Obtaining a driving license: In some cases, it is required to obtain or renew a driving license.  
\- Processing residency: It is a document necessary to apply for residency in Spain.  
\- Voting in municipal elections: To be able to vote in municipal elections.  
\- Other administrative procedures: For a wide variety of other administrative procedures at the municipal level.

How can I obtain registration?

To register, you must go to the town hall of the municipality where you reside. The process generally involves:

1\. Submitting the application: You must fill out a registration application form.  
2\. Submitting the documentation: You must present your DNI or NIE (Foreigner Identification Number), passport and a document proving your address (rental contract, utility bill, etc.).  
3\. Obtaining the certificate: Once the town hall has processed your application, they will give you a registration certificate. In many town halls, this process can be done online.

How long is the registration certificate valid for?

The registration certificate does not have a specific expiration date. However, some agencies may request a recent certificate (less than three months old, for example). If you need the certificate for a specific administrative procedure, it is advisable to check whether there is any time limit on the validity of the document. In the event of a change of address, you must request a new certificate of registration.

In short, registration is an essential registration to prove residence in a Spanish municipality and is necessary for a wide variety of procedures. The registration process is generally simple and is carried out at the corresponding town hall.

**European Residence in Spain**

“European Residence” in Spain refers to the residence certificate issued to citizens of the EU, EEA, or Switzerland who live in Spain for more than three months. It is not a special visa, but official proof of legal residence.

**Purpose of the EU residence card**

The card confirms your legal stay in Spain and makes everyday procedures easier, including:

* Access to the Spanish job market

* Access to public healthcare and education

* Opening bank accounts

* Applying for other permits

* Proving your address for administrative matters

**Is it mandatory?**

If you stay in Spain longer than three months, you must register as a resident. The physical card itself is not strictly mandatory, but it is the easiest and most practical way to prove your legal residence.

**Required documents**

Although requirements may vary slightly by region, you generally need:

* Valid passport or national ID

* Application form

* Proof of address in Spain (e.g., rental contract)

* Recent photograph

* Payment of the relevant fee

**Where to apply and cost**

You apply at the Immigration Office in your province of residence. Fees are usually low, but it’s best to check locally for updated costs and procedures.

**Summary**

While not strictly required in physical form, the EU residence card is highly recommended. It simplifies many administrative processes and serves as official proof of legal residence in Spain.

**Digital Signature**  
A **digital signature** guarantees the authenticity, integrity, and non-repudiation of an electronic document. It confirms that the document has not been altered and that it was signed by the claimed person. Unlike a scanned handwritten signature, it provides much higher security and legal validity.

**What is it used for?**  
Digital signatures are commonly used for:

* Signing online contracts  
* Filing tax returns  
* Signing official administrative documents  
* Authorizing financial transactions  
* Securing electronic communications  
* Accessing secure systems

**Where is it stored?**  
A digital signature is created using a digital certificate. This certificate (which contains the public key) can be stored:

* On a secure hardware device (USB token or smart card)  
* In certificate software on a computer (less secure)

**Who manages it?**  
The user is responsible for protecting their certificate and private key. A Certification Authority (CA) issues and manages the certificate but does not store the private key.

**Who can request it?**  
Anyone needing to verify a document’s authenticity can request a digital signature, but only the owner of the private key can create it.

In short, digital signatures ensure secure and legally valid electronic transactions and must be handled responsibly.

**Utility transcription**   
(more accurately called a *utility transfer*) refers to changing the utility contracts into the new owner’s name after buying a property.

When an immigrant purchases a home, they must transfer services such as electricity, water, and gas from the previous owner to themselves. This ensures:

* The bills are issued in the new owner’s name  
* Responsibility for payments is legally transferred  
* Services continue without interruption

To do this, the buyer usually provides their ID/NIE, property details, bank account number, and a copy of the purchase deed (*escritura*).

It’s a simple but important step after completing the property purchase.
